Buying guide

The best rust-resistant hinges

"Rust-proof" is marketing; "316 stainless" is a spec. Here's how to actually buy hardware that won't streak rust down your door.

What to look for

01

Grade over slogans

Ignore "rust-proof" and look for the grade. 316 is the most corrosion-resistant common choice; 304 is excellent away from salt.

02

Matching fasteners

The most rust-resistant hinge still streaks if you pair it with carbon-steel screws. Match the grade.

03

Finish quality

A clean, well-finished or electropolished surface resists staining better in damp air.

04

Right environment

No hinge is immune everywhere — the "most rust-resistant" choice depends on whether salt is present.

Frequently asked questions

Is any hinge truly rust-proof?

No metal hinge is completely immune in every condition. 316 stainless is the most practical "won't rust" choice for hardware, especially near salt.

Why did my 'rust-proof' hinge rust?

Likely a low grade behind a marketing label, carbon-steel contamination, or mismatched fasteners. Buy by grade, not by slogan.
