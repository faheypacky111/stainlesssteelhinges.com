Industrial hinges guide

Reviewed June 2026 · independent, spec-first guidance

Industrial settings ask hinges to carry serious weight and cycle constantly, sometimes in corrosive air. Here's how to spec for that.

What to look for

01

Load and cycle first

Match per-hinge or per-pair load ratings to the actual door or panel weight with margin, and choose ball-bearing or geared for high cycles.

02

Weld-on vs bolt-on

Weld-on barrel and bullet hinges suit steel fabrication; bolt-on for serviceable installs.

03

Grade for the atmosphere

Wash-down, food, and chemical plants want 316; general plant air is fine with 304.

04

Continuous for abuse

Full-height continuous hinges spread stress on heavy, frequently abused doors and panels.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Plain-bearing hinges on high-cycle equipment.
  • Under-rated hinges on heavy panels.
  • Wrong grade for washdown or chemical areas.
  • Skipping serviceable (greasable or weld-on) hinges where rebuild matters.

Frequently asked questions

Weld-on vs bolt-on industrial hinges?

Weld-on give maximum strength and a clean line on steel; bolt-on allow field replacement. Choose by whether you need serviceability or ultimate rigidity.

When is 316 worth it industrially?

Washdown, food processing, chemical exposure, and coastal facilities — anywhere chlorides or aggressive cleaning attack 304.

304 or 316 for a factory?

General dry plant air: 304. Wash-down, food processing, or chemical exposure: 316.
